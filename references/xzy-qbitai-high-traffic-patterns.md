## Shared High-Spread Features

1. **Title carries the first decision.** A recognizable entity plus number or role shift appears early: OpenAI, Claude, Meta, SpaceX, Momenta, Token, GPU, IPO, Star. Avoid vague category-only titles.
2. **First screen is dense.** Within the first 120 Chinese characters: event, actor, tension, and why it matters. No warm-up essay.
3. **A one-line subtitle or lead compresses the hook.** The lead should be readable as a social-card summary.
4. **Conflict is concrete, not decorative.** Good conflict examples: old leader vs new entrant, free vs paid, GPU vs CPU, demo vs order, model benchmark vs real workflow.
5. **Numbers are used as anchors.** Price, ARR, token volume, Star count, IPO value, order scale, growth multiple, model version, and dates make articles easier to forward.
6. **Scenes beat abstractions.** QbitAI often starts from an actual test, payment, order, road, vehicle, paper, GitHub project, or product screen. Translate abstract AI news into one inspectable scene.
7. **Mechanism follows the hook.** After the dramatic first screen, the article explains how it works: architecture, business loop, model route, product workflow, or supply chain.
8. **Screenshots are evidence.** Images should look like proof: official page, product UI, paper figure, X post, report table, GitHub repo, chart, or order/product photo. Pure decorative images dilute trust.
9. **Short paragraphs and frequent turns.** Use mobile-friendly paragraphs, enough white space, and section headings that name actors or mechanisms.
10. **Closing returns to a decision.** End on what changes for a user, worker, creator, developer, investor, manager, or buyer.

## Research/Data Article Upgrade

When the article is based on a study, report, education result, score change, distribution, before/after comparison, or AI usage effect, use a research-card lead instead of a generic tech-card lead:

- Title should turn the finding into a human reversal, such as `AI正在制造一种“假优秀”学生`, not a neutral report title.
- Opening should start from a familiar scene or question, such as `AI帮你写作业，谁帮你考试？`, before naming the paper.
- Main image should look like evidence: chart, distribution, grouped metrics, source line. It should not look like generic workflow decoration.
- Use `research-card-visual-standard.md` for drawing and QA.

Borrow the concreteness, not the identity. Do not copy `发自凹非寺`, `量子位 | 公众号 QbitAI`, or staff-style bylines.

## Zeelin Application Rules

Use these when drafting every AI/tech news article:

1. Generate title candidates in three lanes before choosing:
   - Collision lane: entity + action/conflict + consequence.
   - Utility lane: verified number + product/workflow + user/business change.
   - Decision lane: what the affected person should now judge or do.
2. Pick the title with the clearest sourced anchor. Never add fake numbers or urgency.
3. The opening paragraph must put actor + event + changed decision in the first 120 Chinese characters.
4. Each section should contain one evidence sentence and one interpretation sentence. If a section has only opinion, add a source-backed fact or cut it.
5. Add one concrete scene before abstract judgment: product screen, payment tier, order, job workflow, repo, paper, report, benchmark, or user task.
6. Use at least one “not A, but B” conceptual turn when it is true and sourced. Do not label it as a trope in public text.
7. Use visuals as evidence, not decoration. Put one source visual near the first third of the article.
8. Keep the ending practical: what changes in cost, workflow, buying, learning, job risk, compliance, or product choice.
9. Keep Zeelin's anti-hallucination layer stronger than the reference outlets: uncertain claims are softened, removed, or placed in `事实来源` context.
